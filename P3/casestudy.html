Case Study:

HR. MacMillian Space Centre

About HR. MacMillian Space Centre

H.R. MacMillan Space Centre is a non-profit community resource that brings the wonders of space to Earth, while providing a personal sense of ongoing discovery.

HR. MacMillian Space

Our Goals

Our goal was to examine and understand the Space Centre's target customer group solve user frustrations based on our research and data. Assisting the Space centre in developing and improving its current projects.

Step #1: Research

Design Requirements based on Research

#1

The design should enhance the visitors’ experience at the Space Center.

#2

The design should help the marketing team promoting the Space Center and reaching out to new customer groups.

#3

The design should require minimal changes/new instalments at the Space Center.

Survey Questions

4 people on-site visit the Space center for 3 hours on Sunday which includes gallery exploration and participation in 2 events. The primary collection methods are by observation of the visitors and participation as visitors. Additionally, asked survey questions to the front desk employee and online research of H.R. MacMillan Space Centre online presence through social media (Facebook, Twitter, and Youtube).

Thematic Analysis

Visual Representation

The H.R. MacMillan Space Centre is all about learning knowledge on space and astronomy. However, there is a lot of text presented all at once at the gallery which could scare visitors away and or overload them with information. The experience could be improved by transforming into points from short paragraphs on an interactive screen.

Online Presence

The most effective & efficient way of promoting an organization and spreading the name is by social media. Whether it’d be Facebook, Twitter, or Youtube channel, are all good platforms to spread the word. Currently, the H.R. MacMillan Space Centre is lacking effective forms of promoting their events, program, and services etc. on their Social Media.

Website UI/UX

The H.R. MacMillan Space Centre’s website lacks effective presentation for the visitors to intuitively understand what is offered. The landing page does not provide much information about the Space Centre (events, what visitors can do, what the museum is about etc.). Most of the content requires deep exploration into the website. The navigations and dropdown menu also lacks clear visual guidance.

Gallery Organization

The gallery at the Space Center presents a lot of knowledge and a wide range of topics (from alien’s existence, to Earth’s geographic, then to star types etc.). But the presentation feels unorganized with different topics mending into each other’s section (if there is one). Thus, the gallery could be improved with a better visual border that organizes different topics into respective sections.

Step 2: Personas

Discovering Personas

#1: Tourists & Young Adults

Tourists & Young adults seeks for fun, enjoyment, and excitement. They want to explore new things and share it with their family and friends.

#2: Parents

Parents bring their kids to museums/planetariums mostly because the parents themselves have an interest in space and believes that it is beneficial for their kids to learn about.

#3 Teachers

Teachers are attracted by opportunities that would bring meaningful experiences to their students, and invoking interests.
